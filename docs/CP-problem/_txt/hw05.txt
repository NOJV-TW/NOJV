    5.5       Turing Machines exists!!! (20 pts)
    Panda is a CSIE student, he believes that Turing Machines exists!
    But he is bad at programming, so he wants you to solve a Turing Machine
    Problem for him.
    Here is the problem statement: There are 2 tapes (imagine them as 2 mem-
    ories) and 2 pointers pointing to the start of the tape.
    The first tape consists of 2 arrays x and y split by -1, which looks like
    [−1, x1 , x2 , . . . , xn , −1, y1 , y2 , . . . , ym , −1].
    The second tape is structured like [−1, −1, . . . ]
    Notice that -1 represents a ”split mark” here for simplicity. Meaning that
    if you put something on tape 2 and move right, you will point to another ”-
    1”.(Which means that you don’t need to worry about allocating undeclared
    memory in tapes.)
    Now, your task is to make convert the second tape into the following struc-
    ture: [−1, x0 y0 , x0 y1 , . . . , x0 ym , . . . , xn , ym ]
    For example:
    Tape 1 has the following data: ” − 1, 0, 1, 0, −1, 1, 2, 3, 4, −1”. (x=”010”,
    y=”1234”)
    Tape 2 should be converted into ” − 1, 0, 0, 0, 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 0, 0, 0, 0, −1” after
    going through the Turing machine.
    You will need to implement the following functions:
1 \\ ptape1 points to x1 , and ptape2 points to the first position
      of tape 2 by default .
2 \\ I promise that the inputs are all valid .
3 \\ You are NOT allowed to declare any variable .
4 \\ x is in {0 ,1} , y is in [0 ,9].

5 \\ tape 1 should restore to its opriginal state after running .
6 \\ pointers should point to the beginning of tapes . (not the
     first -1, it is a "wall" here)
7 void TuringMachine ( uint8_t * ptape1 , uint8_t * ptape2 );


    Hint:
    1. You can use ptape++ or ptape– to move the pointer right or left.
    2. You can ”mark” a location in tape 1 by adding 10 and ”unmark” by
    subtracting 10.
    3. It is recommended for you to create something like an automata.
